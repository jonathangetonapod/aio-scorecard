🎯 Full Methodology

🚨 Why This Matters

B2B buyers are increasingly using AI to find vendors and evaluate options:

  • ChatGPT has 200M+ weekly users asking questions like "who's the best X manufacturer?"
  • Perplexity is replacing Google for research and sourcing queries
  • When AI doesn't mention you, buyers go straight to the competitors it recommends instead - they never even see your website

📍 Step 1: Auto-Detect From Domain

We scrape your website and use AI to automatically extract:

  • Company Name: Pulled from your site title and page content
  • Primary Keyword: Your most specific, searchable capability (e.g., "investment casting", "5-axis CNC machining")
  • Secondary Keywords: Other processes, materials, or certifications you offer
  • Vertical: Your industry category (Aerospace, Medical Devices, Automotive, CNC Machining, etc.)

Why auto-detect? We use AI to find the specific keywords buyers actually search for - not generic marketing terms. Just enter a domain and everything else is detected.

🔍 Step 2: Real Buyer-Intent Queries

We query both ChatGPT and Perplexity with real buyer-intent questions built from your detected keywords:

Query Type Example
Research "What companies offer [keyword] services?"
Quote "I need [keyword] for a project - who should I contact?"
Supplier "Best [keyword] suppliers for [vertical] applications"
Compare "Compare the top [keyword] companies"

We run 8+ queries per platform (16+ total) to get statistically meaningful results.

📊 Step 3: Analysis and Results

For each AI response, we check:

  • Did the AI mention your company name or domain? Your domain is highlighted in green wherever it appears.
  • Which competitors were mentioned instead? We identify and rank every company AI recommends.
  • What did the AI actually say? You can read the full response for every query, not just a snippet.

This gives you your visibility score, a competitor comparison, and a clear picture of who is winning in AI search for your keywords.

📉 Why Scores Are Often Low

Don't panic if you score under 30% - that's normal:

  • Most B2B websites aren't optimized for AI discovery
  • AI favors companies with strong online authority (directories, case studies, reviews, structured data)
  • Niche and mid-market companies are harder for AI to find than large national brands

The opportunity: Your competitors are likely scoring low too. Companies that optimize for AI visibility now will dominate this channel early.
